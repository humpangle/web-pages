General Information
Who should complete this application
You must have received the letter of intent to reside in Quebec during COVID-19 from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada
(IRCC) inviting you to submit an application to the MIFI and, as the case may be:
• Have qualifying employment in Canada for a period of at least 750 hours before 1 st September 2021, at least
120 hours of effective occupation between March 13 and August 14, 2020.
OR
• Be the widow or widower of a deceased refugee claimant from COVID-19 who has held an eligible job in Canada
between March 13 and August 14, 2020.
Eligible jobs
Eligible jobs in the Special Program for Asylum Seekers During COVID-19 (PSDAPC) must have been completed
specifically in the health sector. According to the National Occupational Classification, the list of eligible jobs is as follows:
• Nursing co-ordinators and supervisors (CNP code 3011);
• Registered nurses / registered psychiatric nurses / registered psychiatric nurses (CNP code 3012);
• Practitioners connected in primary health care (code 3124);
• Nursing assistants (CNP code 3233);
• Nursing aides, orderlies and orderlies (CNP code 3413);
• Resident family helpers / resident family helpers, home support workers and related personnel (CNP code 4412), but
only in so far as they relate to the main performance of one or more of the following functions:
- provide care to people during periods of incapacity, convalescence or family crisis;
- provide bedside and personal care to people, including helping them to walk, bathe, take care of themselves
their personal hygiene, dressing and undressing ;
- giving routine medical care, including changing non-sterile dressings, helping to give medication
and take samples, under the general direction of a supervisor of the home care service or a
nurse.
An internship carried out in the health sector as part of a study program leading to employment is considered to be an eligible job.

• the original of the complete translation made by a recognized translator . To know the requirements related to

Definitions
National occupational classification
The National Occupational Classification (CNP) is a system used to describe occupations in Canada and Quebec. It provides, for
500 occupations found in the labor market, a standardized description of each occupation and the nature of the work it
covers ( https://www.canada.ca/fr/immigration-refugies-citoyennete/services/immigrer-canada/entree-express/admissibilite/trouver-
classification-nationale-professions.html) .
Effective occupation
Effective occupation of a job means that you must have actually worked the hours you claim. For example,
parental or medical leave cannot be included in the calculation of the period of effective occupation of a job. The only exception
possible is withdrawal from the workplace because you have contracted COVID-19 or have been the subject of a measure aimed at it
prevention.
Qualifying internship
An eligible internship must consist primarily of providing direct patient and elder care. He may have been paid
or not. It must have been carried out in the healthcare sector, in one of the following situations:
• as part of a study program leading to employment eligible for the PSDAPC;
• in order to meet the requirements relating to the exercise of an eligible job, when this job is a profession governed by an order
professional from Canada.

14. Declaration on democratic values ​​and expressed Quebec values
by the Charter of human rights and freedoms
Quebec is a democratic francophone nation that welcomes immigrants from all over the world.
These people enrich Quebec with their aspirations, skills and know-how. They contribute
to the development of their community.
In Quebec, everyone must obey the laws. Every Quebecer enjoys recognized rights and freedoms
by the Charter of human rights and freedoms and other laws, which express the values ​​specific to Quebec society.
In 2019, the National Assembly of Quebec adopted the Law on the Secularism of the State in order to affirm that the State of Quebec
is secular.
Quebec society is also governed by the Charter of the French language which makes French the language of the State and of the law as well.
than the normal and usual language of work, education, communications, commerce and business.
Learning democratic values ​​and Quebec values ​​expressed by the Charter of Rights and Freedoms of
person is a first step towards the successful integration of immigrants. The main values ​​that
founding Quebec society are as follows:
• in Quebec, women and men are equal;
• Quebec is a free and democratic society;
• Quebec is a secular society;
• Quebec is a francophone society;
• Quebec is a society based on the rule of law;
• Quebec is a society where the exercise of human rights and freedoms must be carried out with respect for those of others
and general well-being;
• Quebec is a pluralist society.
